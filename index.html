Cajun Shrimp Fettucini Alfredo

  • Time: 35 minutes
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
  • Fettucini noodles
  • Cajun Seasoning
  • Shrimp
  • Butter
  • Olive Oil
  • Minced Garlic
  • Salt
  • Parsley
  • Parmesean Cheese
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Reserved Pasta Water
  • 8oz
  • 1 Tablespoon
  • 1 lb medium shrimp peeled and clean
  • 4 Tablespoons
  • 1 Teaspoon
  • 1 Tablespoon
  • 1 Teaspoon- or to taste
  • To taste
  • 1 Cup grated
  • 3 Tablespoons
  • 2 Cups
  • 2 cups

Step 1:Cook fettuccine noodles according to package directions. Drain (*Reserve 2 cups pasta water*), toss with a bit of olive oil, and set aside.
Step 2:In a large skillet melt 1 tablespoon butter. Stir in 1 teaspoon olive oil.
Step 3:Add shrimp to the skillet and sprinkle Cajun seasoning all over the shrimp, then stir for 4-7 minutes until shrimp is opaque.
Step 4:Use tongs or a slotted spoon to transfer shrimp to a dish and cover to keep warm.
Step 5:Without cleaning out the skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in garlic for 1 minute til fragrant.
Step 6:Stir in flour for 1 minute. Gradually whisk in milk, then reserved pasta water.
Step 7:Add parmesan cheese and salt and stir until parmesan is dissolved. Taste, add salt and pepper if needed.
Step 8:Add cooked fettuccine noodles to the skillet and return shrimp to the skillet. Give it a good toss.
Step 9:Garnish with chopped parsley and additional grated parmesan if desired, and serve.

Whole Fried Snapper

Ingredients Quantity
  • Red Snappers
  • Lime Juice
  • Minced Garlic
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Parsley
  • Flour
  • 4, make sure they are cleaned
  • 4 Tablespoon
  • 3 Tablespoons
  • 1/4 Teaspoon
  • 1/2 Tablespoon
  • 1/2 Teaspoon
  • 1 Teaspoon
  • 2 Tablespoons
  • 2 Cups

Step 1:Rinse snappers well with water.
Step 2:On a cutting board, create 3 deep slits on both sides of the snappers.
Step 3:Place snappers in a bowl as you go.
Step 4:When done, we are going to season.
Pour lime juice, garlic, pepper, salt, oregano, onion powder and adobo over the fish and mix in well into the fish.
Step 5:Cover and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours but overnight would be preferred. I have even seasoned for 36 hours. The flavors get super into the fish. Yum!
Step 6:When ready to make, use the largest non-stick frying pan you have. Add enough oil to cover at least 1-1/2 inches of depth. Step 7:Heat the skillet for a few minutes over medium heat. In the meantime, dredge the snapper.
Step 8:Dredge in flour and make sure to get flour into those crevices so they are also nice and crispy.
Step 9:Fry for a few minutes on the first side but do not turn until it is ready. Just check by lifting a little to make sure the snapper is golden. That is a sign, especially at medium heat that is is ready to be turned over.
Step 10:Once you turn over, dredge the cooked side with the oil a couple of times to make sure the top stays nice and hot.
Step 11:Total time should be no longer than 15 minutes for frying. But of course, not all stoves are the same so keep an eye on it the entire time.
Step 12:Serve with your favorite sides.

Cannoli

Ingredients Quantity
  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Shortening
  • Cinnamon
  • Marsala Wine
  • Water
  • Distilled Vinegar
  • Egg
  • Yolk
  • Egg White
  • Oil
  • Ricotta Cheese
  • Confectioners Sugar
  • Semisweet Chocolate
  • Lemon Zest
  • 3 Cups
  • 1/4 Cup
  • 3 Tablespoons
  • 1/4 Teaspoon
  • 1/2 Cup
  • 2 Tablespoons
  • 1 Tablespoon
  • 1 Large
  • 1
  • 1
  • 1 Quart
  • 32 oz Drained
  • 1/2 Cup
  • 4 oz Chopped
  • 1 Teaspoon

Step 1: Make shells: Mix flour, sugar, and cinnamon together in a medium bowl. Cut in shortening until crumbly.
Step 2: Make a well in the center and add Marsala wine, water, vinegar, egg, and egg yolk.
Step 3: Mix with a fork until the dough becomes stiff, then finish kneading it by hand on a clean surface, adding a bit more water if needed for about 10 minutes.
Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours.
Step 4: Divide cannoli dough into three balls; flatten each one just enough to get through the pasta machine. Roll a ball of dough through successively thinner settings until you have reached the thinnest setting. Dust lightly with flour if necessary.
Step 5: Place the sheet of dough on a lightly floured surface. Using a cutter or large glass, cut out 4 to 5-inch circles.
Step 6: Dust the circles with a light coating of flour. This will help you later in removing the shells from the tubes. Roll dough around cannoli tubes, sealing the edge with a bit of egg white. Repeat with remaining dough balls.
Step 7: Heat oil in a deep fryer or deep skillet to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
Step 8: Fry shells on the tubes in hot oil, a few at a time, until golden, about 2 to 3 minutes. Use tongs to turn as needed. Remove shells carefully using tongs, and place them on a cooling rack set over paper towels.
Step 9: Cool just long enough that you can handle the tubes, then carefully twist the tube to remove the shell. Using a tea towel may help you get a better grip. Wash or wipe off the tubes, and use them for more shells.
Cooled shells can be placed in an airtight container and kept for up to 2 months. You should only fill them immediately or up to 1 hour before serving.
Step 10: Make filling: Mix ricotta cheese and confectioners' sugar together in a large bowl until well combined. Fold in chocolate and lemon zest.
Step 11:Transfer mixture into a pastry bag and pipe into shells, filling from the center to one end, then doing the same from the other side. Dust with additional confectioners' sugar to serve, if you like.
